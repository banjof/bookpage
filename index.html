For as long as Florence Bamidele Makanjuola could recollect, her doting father had spoken glowingly of another Florence, who lived in a different lifetime, years before her birth. A Florence, full of good works and compassion for others. A spark was lit in little Florence’s heart that would last her lifetime. Florence Makanjuola found a role model in Florence Nightingale, the mother of modern nursing, and was determined to be a nurse too. Just when this dream was about to become a reality, it was nearly derailed. Yet, providence stepped in and put it back on track.

Her father’s sudden passing abruptly conferred on her the leadership of the family. Her husband’s prolonged illness and eventual demise, and several other events undoubtedly tell of strength of character, courage, love and tenacity.

Born into the royal family of Akegun in Imesi-Ile, Osun State, Nigeria, Florence Bamidele Makanjuola was an accomplished teacher, nurse and beloved matriarch. In this slim volume, she compressed over nine decades of an extraordinary life. Testimonials from family, friends and well-wishers add colour to the character bringing to light her life of exemplary impact.

Florence: Daughter of Akegun is a simple, but powerful story about life in all its colors, hope, love, loss, family, sacrifice, and triumph.
